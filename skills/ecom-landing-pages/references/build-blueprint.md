# Build Blueprint

Heavy detail for the ideation skill: the full archetype taxonomy, the
section-by-section page anatomy, reusable copy patterns, archetype-specific
structural deltas, and the pre-launch checklist. Load this when generating a
concept spread or building a chosen page.

---

## The 8-archetype taxonomy (full)

Every landing page is one of these angles. During ideation, walk each archetype
against the intake and ask "is there a page here?" One page can stack
archetypes — lead with the dominant one, because the headline gets to make
exactly one promise. The examples are live Mill.com pages.

| # | Archetype | Definition | When to reach for it | Mill examples |
|---|-----------|------------|----------------------|---------------|
| 1 | **Audience / persona** | Speaks to one identity group; the headline names them | A distinct buyer segment with its own motivation | `/lp/chickens` ("Feed your flock"), `/lp/offices`, `/lp/commercial` |
| 2 | **Use-case** | Built around one job the product does | The product solves several jobs; a segment cares about one | `/lp/food-grounds`, `/lp/gardens-archive`, `/lp/chickens` (as treats) |
| 3 | **Pain / emotional reframe** | Leads with a felt problem or emotion, then resolves it | Cold traffic that doesn't know the category | `/lp/busy-schedule` ("Save time — and the planet"), `/lp/one-easy-thing` (climate guilt → empowerment) |
| 4 | **Location / geo** | Localized to a city or region; local proof + local logistics | Geo-targeted ads, local programs, regional PR | `/lp/nyc`, `/lp/la`, `/lp/nyc-curbside-compost` |
| 5 | **Event / seasonal / promo** | Tied to a date, sale, or in-person moment; urgency-driven | A campaign window or a live event | `/lp/memorial-day-sale`, `/lp/event-tabling`, `/lp/big-night` |
| 6 | **Offer / trial / guarantee** | Leads with risk-reversal or a specific offer | Conversion-stage traffic that needs a nudge | `/lp/free-trial`, `/lp/90-day-mbg`, `/lp/event-two-month-trial` |
| 7 | **Occasion / gift** | Positions the product as a gift; reframes the buyer | Holiday and gifting seasons | `/lp/gifting` ("The gift that ends food waste") |
| 8 | **Segment / B2B + partner** | Institutional buyer or referral partner; metrics + process, not lifestyle | Long-cycle B2B, or affiliate recruitment | `/lp/municipalities`, `/lp/offices`, affiliate/trade program pages |

**Stacking:** a single page can combine two angles (geo + use-case, offer +
event). Name both, but lead the headline with the one that best matches the
traffic. Pages that try to lead with two promises convert on neither.

## The idea matrix

Cross the intake against the taxonomy to produce candidates:

1. For each audience, pain, use-case, geo, offer, event, and segment from
   intake, draft a one-line concept under the matching archetype.
2. Anchor each to a traffic source (see the SKILL body — traffic source is the
   angle selector). The source decides the angle and the headline's job.
3. Don't stop at the obvious. A single food-recycler product became pages for
   chickens, gardens, gifting, municipalities, and an affiliate program. Push
   every archetype at least once before ranking.

## Page anatomy (10-section skeleton)

Once a concept is chosen, build it on this skeleton. Order matters: it walks a
cold visitor from hook → proof → mechanics → trust → action.

1. **Hero** — the only part guaranteed to be seen.
   - **Headline:** one benefit-first promise, tuned to the angle.
   - **Subheadline:** expands the promise; names the mechanism or removes the
     top objection.
   - **Primary CTA:** one action verb, one goal ("Shop now" / "Get started").
   - **Proof in the hero where possible:** price ("From $799"), rating ("5
     stars, 2,000 reviews"), or a marquee award. Offer/promo pages put price +
     rating above the fold.
2. **Benefit pillars (3):** the three reasons to care, scannable, icon + line
   each. Three is the pattern; resist a fourth.
3. **How it works (3–4 steps):** demystify the mechanic. Reduces perceived risk
   for an unfamiliar category.
4. **Use-cases:** show the product in the visitor's specific context. On an
   audience/use-case page, go deep on their job here.
5. **Social proof, tiered** — strongest-to-relatable, stacked:
   - **Awards** (an industry "Most Innovative" nod)
   - **Press logos** ("Featured in": major outlets)
   - **Verified customer testimonials** (named, verified-buyer badge; quote the
     objection they had and how it resolved)
   - **Influencer / authority endorsement**
6. **Risk reversal:** the friction-remover, given its own section (trial,
   warranty, free shipping/returns). State it plainly near the decision point.
7. **Comparison (when there's a choice):** if there are tiers or models, a
   side-by-side table with a "Bestseller" flag steers the decision.
8. **FAQ:** absorb the remaining objections so they don't become un-clicks.
9. **Cross-sell / expansion:** soft links to adjacent use-cases or products for
   the not-yet-ready.
10. **Final CTA:** repeat the primary CTA. A visitor who scrolled to the bottom
    is ready; don't make them scroll back up.

## Core principles

- **Message match / scent.** The ad's promise and the page headline must be
  visibly continuous. A "feed your chickens" ad lands on a page that says
  chickens in the first line. Break scent and the click bounces.
- **One conversion goal.** One primary CTA, repeated. No competing asks, minimal
  nav, no site chrome pulling attention sideways.
- **Proof early and often.** Awards and ratings above the fold; testimonials
  before the final ask.
- **Pre-handle objections.** Name the doubt before the visitor does. If people
  don't buy because of smell, mess, and fruit flies, address smell, mess, and
  fruit flies repeatedly.
- **Scannable.** Headline + three pillars + one verified quote should carry the
  whole pitch to a visitor who reads nothing else.

## Copy patterns (observed, reusable)

- **Benefit-first headline with a twist:** "Save time — and the planet." "Make
  food. Not waste." Promise plus a small surprise.
- **Name the audience in the headline:** "Feed your flock." Instant relevance
  for an audience page.
- **Pain → empowerment reframe:** acknowledge overwhelm, then hand over agency.
  "Sometimes it seems like there's nothing we can do. But there's a way to take
  action every day."
- **Sensory objection pre-handling:** for physical products, name and kill the
  gross or risky associations early (odor, mess, flies, noise).
- **Anti-category framing for gifts:** "Don't give them more clutter." Reframe
  the buyer's decision, not just the product.

## Archetype-specific structural deltas

Start from the 10-section skeleton, then adjust for the angle:

- **B2B / segment:** swap lifestyle copy for **quantified metrics** ("80%
  reduction," diversion/efficiency stats) and **case studies** (named
  cities or accounts). Primary CTA is soft and lead-gen ("Stay in the loop,"
  "Get in touch") feeding a CRM or pipeline, not a cart. Longer cycle,
  decision-maker audience.
- **Promo / seasonal:** price, discount, and urgency move **up top**. Put the
  offer terms and any deadline near the hero; the benefit education can compress.
- **Occasion / gift:** reframe the reader as the giver; anti-clutter / "leaves
  an impression" framing; handle gift logistics (delivery timing, gift
  messaging).
- **Geo:** local social proof (local customers or influencers), local logistics
  (curbside programs, pickup availability), and city-named copy throughout.
- **Partner / affiliate:** no product feature dump. Lead with **who you partner
  with**, **how to join** (3 steps), and **terms** (commission, cookie window,
  payout cadence). CTA points to the external affiliate network, not checkout.

## Pre-launch checklist

Gate every page on this before it ships:

- [ ] **One goal:** a single primary CTA, repeated; no competing asks.
- [ ] **Message match:** the headline visibly continues the ad or email that
      drives the traffic.
- [ ] **Above the fold:** clear promise + CTA + at least one proof element
      without scrolling.
- [ ] **Proof present:** awards/press, two or more verified testimonials,
      risk-reversal stated.
- [ ] **Objections handled:** the top 3 purchase doubts addressed in copy or FAQ.
- [ ] **Mobile:** hero, CTA, and proof work on a phone; tap targets are real.
- [ ] **Fast:** images optimized; no render-blocking junk. A slow page burns
      paid spend.
- [ ] **Tracking:** the conversion event fires; UTM/source attribution is wired
      so the page can be judged.
- [ ] **CTA destination correct:** cart, checkout, lead form, or external network
      as intended.
- [ ] **Nav stripped:** no full site header/footer competing for the click. This
      is a landing page, not a store page.
